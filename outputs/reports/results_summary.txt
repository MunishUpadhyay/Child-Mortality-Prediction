Interestingly, economically developed states such as Gujarat and Maharashtra also appear in high-risk categories, suggesting that economic growth does not directly translate into improved child health outcomes.”
“A weighted composite risk index was developed to reflect the relative importance of acute and chronic malnutrition indicators.”
“After incorporating weighted importance of malnutrition indicators, the model produced more stable and realistic rankings, reducing distortions caused by smaller regions and emphasizing chronic and acute nutritional risks.”
“A Random Forest classifier was selected due to its superior performance and stability compared to a Decision Tree model. Cross-validation results showed a mean accuracy of approximately 89%, indicating reliable classification despite the limited dataset size.”
"This study analyzed child mortality risk across Indian states using NFHS data and machine learning techniques. The results indicate that malnutrition, particularly stunting and wasting, is the dominant determinant of child mortality risk, contributing approximately 68% of the overall importance in the predictive model. Socio-economic factors such as female literacy and sanitation also play a meaningful role, while immunization coverage, although important, shows comparatively lower influence when considered alongside nutritional indicators. These findings suggest that addressing malnutrition should be prioritized over isolated healthcare interventions to achieve substantial reductions in child mortality."
“India has witnessed a substantial decline in under-5 mortality over the past decades, indicating improvements in healthcare and policy interventions.”
“Although India has achieved a remarkable ~90% reduction in under-5 mortality over time, significant disparities persist across states due to malnutrition and socio-economic inequalities.”
“The dominance of communicable, maternal, perinatal, and nutritional causes (≈80%) in SRS data strongly aligns with the machine learning findings, where stunting and wasting emerged as the most significant predictors of child mortality risk.”
“Despite a significant decline in child mortality over time, a large proportion of deaths continue to be driven by malnutrition and related factors, which vary significantly across states.”
